-----------------------------------------------
SECTION 1: CONTINUOUS-TIME FOURIER TRANSFORM (CTFT)
-----------------------------------------------

ANALYSIS EQUATION (Forward Transform):
The CTFT of a CT signal x(t) is defined as:

        X(j*omega) = Integral from -infinity to infinity of
                     x(t) * exp(-j*omega*t) dt

This decomposes x(t) into its constituent complex exponential
frequency components, where omega is real-valued angular frequency
(radians/second), omega in (-infinity, infinity).

SYNTHESIS EQUATION (Inverse Transform):
The original signal is recovered from its CTFT via:

        x(t) = (1/(2*pi)) * Integral from -infinity to infinity of
               X(j*omega) * exp(j*omega*t) d(omega)

EXISTENCE NOTE: The CTFT exists (converges) for any x(t) that is
absolutely integrable (Integral of |x(t)| dt < infinity), satisfies
the Dirichlet conditions, or more generally for signals of finite
energy. For certain signals that are not absolutely integrable but
are bounded and of practical interest (e.g., delta(t), u(t),
sinusoids), the CTFT exists in a generalized sense, typically
involving impulse functions in the frequency domain.

-----------------------------------------------
SECTION 2: DISCRETE-TIME FOURIER TRANSFORM (DTFT)
-----------------------------------------------

ANALYSIS EQUATION (Forward Transform):
The DTFT of a DT signal x[n] is defined as:

        X(e^(j*omega)) = Sum from n = -infinity to infinity of
                          x[n] * exp(-j*omega*n)

SYNTHESIS EQUATION (Inverse Transform):
The original sequence is recovered via integration over exactly one
period of length 2*pi:

        x[n] = (1/(2*pi)) * Integral from -pi to pi of
               X(e^(j*omega)) * exp(j*omega*n) d(omega)

CRITICAL PROPERTY — PERIODICITY OF THE DTFT:
Unlike the CTFT, the DTFT X(e^(j*omega)) is ALWAYS PERIODIC IN omega
WITH PERIOD 2*pi:

        X(e^(j*(omega + 2*pi))) = X(e^(j*omega))      for all omega

This periodicity arises directly from the fact that exp(-j*omega*n)
is itself periodic in omega with period 2*pi for every integer n
(since exp(-j*(omega+2*pi)*n) = exp(-j*omega*n)*exp(-j*2*pi*n) =
exp(-j*omega*n), because exp(-j*2*pi*n) = 1 for integer n). As a
direct consequence, the synthesis (inverse DTFT) integral need only be
evaluated over any single period of length 2*pi (commonly -pi to pi,
or 0 to 2*pi); integrating beyond this range is redundant.

EXISTENCE NOTE: The DTFT converges (in the ordinary sense) for any
absolutely summable sequence (Sum of |x[n]| < infinity), and in the
mean-square sense for any finite-energy (square-summable) sequence.

-----------------------------------------------
SECTION 3: CORE PROPERTIES OF THE FOURIER TRANSFORMS
-----------------------------------------------

Notation: x(t) <---> X(j*omega) denotes a CTFT pair.
          x[n] <---> X(e^(j*omega)) denotes a DTFT pair.

--- 3.1 LINEARITY ---

CTFT:    a*x_1(t) + b*x_2(t)  <--->  a*X_1(j*omega) + b*X_2(j*omega)

DTFT:    a*x_1[n] + b*x_2[n]  <--->  a*X_1(e^(j*omega)) + b*X_2(e^(j*omega))

for arbitrary complex constants a, b.

--- 3.2 TIME SHIFTING ---

CTFT:    x(t - t_0)  <--->  exp(-j*omega*t_0) * X(j*omega)

A time shift introduces a linear PHASE SHIFT in the frequency domain
(of slope -t_0) while leaving the magnitude |X(j*omega)| unchanged,
since |exp(-j*omega*t_0)| = 1.

DTFT:    x[n - n_0]  <--->  exp(-j*omega*n_0) * X(e^(j*omega))

Identical interpretation: only phase is modified, magnitude is
preserved.

--- 3.3 FREQUENCY SHIFTING (MODULATION) ---

CTFT:    exp(j*omega_0*t) * x(t)  <--->  X(j*(omega - omega_0))

DTFT:    exp(j*omega_0*n) * x[n]  <--->  X(e^(j*(omega - omega_0)))

Multiplying a signal by a complex exponential in the time domain
shifts (translates) its spectrum in the frequency domain. This is the
mathematical basis of amplitude modulation (AM).

--- 3.4 DUALITY (CTFT ONLY) ---

The CTFT exhibits a symmetric duality between the time and frequency
domains not shared (in the same form) by the DTFT, owing to the DTFT's
inherent periodicity and the CTFT's pair of unbounded continuous
domains. If:

        x(t)  <--->  X(j*omega)

then the DUALITY PROPERTY states:

        X(j*t)  <--->  2*pi * x(-omega)

That is, if a time function has the same form as a known transform
X(j*omega), the transform of that time function can be found by
substituting into the original x(t) (reversed and scaled by 2*pi).
Example application: since rect(t) <---> 2*sinc(omega/2)-type pulse
relationships, duality lets sinc-shaped time functions be transformed
by inspection from known rectangular-pulse transform pairs.

--- 3.5 DIFFERENTIATION IN TIME ---

CTFT:    d/dt [x(t)]  <--->  (j*omega) * X(j*omega)

This follows directly by differentiating both sides of the CTFT
synthesis equation with respect to t under the integral sign.

DTFT (DIFFERENCING, the DT analog of differentiation):
        x[n] - x[n-1]  <--->  (1 - exp(-j*omega)) * X(e^(j*omega))

(Note: true "differentiation in time" is not defined for discrete
sequences; first-differencing is the standard DT analog and is
included here for completeness. The DTFT also possesses a true dual
property, "differentiation in frequency," given by:
        n * x[n]  <--->  j * d/d(omega) [X(e^(j*omega))] )

--- 3.6 CONVOLUTION PROPERTY ---

This is one of the most important properties for LTI system analysis:
convolution in the time domain corresponds to multiplication in the
frequency domain.

CTFT:
        y(t) = x(t) * h(t)   <--->   Y(j*omega) = X(j*omega) * H(j*omega)

where "*" on the left denotes time-domain convolution:
        x(t) * h(t) = Integral from -infinity to infinity of
                       x(tau) * h(t - tau) d(tau)
and "*" on the right denotes ordinary multiplication of the two
transforms.

DTFT:
        y[n] = x[n] * h[n]   <--->   Y(e^(j*omega)) = X(e^(j*omega)) * H(e^(j*omega))

where the left-hand "*" denotes DT convolution:
        x[n] * h[n] = Sum from k = -infinity to infinity of
                       x[k] * h[n - k]

This property is the foundation of LTI system frequency-response
analysis: the output spectrum equals the input spectrum multiplied by
the system's frequency response H(j*omega) or H(e^(j*omega)).

A DUAL property also holds: multiplication in time corresponds to
convolution (scaled by 1/(2*pi)) in frequency:

CTFT:    x(t) * y(t) [pointwise product]  <--->
              (1/(2*pi)) * [X(j*omega) * Y(j*omega)] (frequency convolution)

DTFT:    x[n] * y[n] [pointwise product]  <--->
              (1/(2*pi)) * Integral over one period of
              X(e^(j*theta)) * Y(e^(j*(omega - theta))) d(theta)
              (periodic convolution over any 2*pi interval)

-----------------------------------------------
SECTION 4: ESSENTIAL TRANSFORM PAIRS LOOKUP TABLE
-----------------------------------------------

--- 4.1 CONTINUOUS-TIME PAIRS ---

(1) Unit Impulse:
        delta(t)   <--->   1
    (The impulse contains all frequencies with equal, unit magnitude.)

(2) DC (Constant) Signal:
        1   <--->   2*pi * delta(omega)
    (A constant in time is concentrated entirely at omega = 0 in
    frequency; this is the dual of pair (1), consistent with the
    duality property in Section 3.4.)

(3) Unit Step Function:
        u(t)   <--->   1/(j*omega) + pi * delta(omega)
    (The unit step is decomposed conceptually as a DC component, which
    is why the delta(omega) term with weight pi appears, plus an
    odd/transient-like component 1/(j*omega).)

(4) Real (Causal) Exponential Decay:
        exp(-a*t) * u(t)   <--->   1 / (a + j*omega)
    VALID CONDITION: Real(a) > 0 (i.e., Re{a} > 0), required for the
    defining integral to converge.

-- ADDITIONAL STANDARD CTFT PAIRS --

(5) Complex Exponential (single frequency):
        exp(j*omega_0*t)   <--->   2*pi * delta(omega - omega_0)

(6) Cosine:
        cos(omega_0*t)   <--->   pi * [delta(omega - omega_0) + delta(omega + omega_0)]

(7) Sine:
        sin(omega_0*t)   <--->   (pi/j) * [delta(omega - omega_0) - delta(omega + omega_0)]
                           equivalently:
                           -j*pi * [delta(omega - omega_0) - delta(omega + omega_0)]

(8) Signum function:
        sgn(t)   <--->   2 / (j*omega)

--- 4.2 DISCRETE-TIME PAIRS ---

(1) Discrete Unit Impulse:
        delta[n]   <--->   1

(2) Discrete Unit Step:
        u[n]   <--->   1 / (1 - exp(-j*omega)) +
                        pi * Sum over k from -infinity to infinity of
                             delta(omega - 2*pi*k)

    NOTE ON STRUCTURE: as in the CT case, this pair decomposes into a
    "non-impulsive" geometric-series-like term 1/(1 - exp(-j*omega))
    plus an impulse train of weight pi, repeated at every integer
    multiple of 2*pi (k = ..., -1, 0, 1, ...), which is REQUIRED so
    that the overall expression respects the mandatory 2*pi-periodicity
    of every DTFT (see Section 2). A single isolated impulse term
    (without the summation over all k) would violate periodicity and
    is therefore NOT a valid DTFT.

-- ADDITIONAL STANDARD DTFT PAIRS --

(3) Real Geometric Sequence (causal):
        a^n * u[n]   <--->   1 / (1 - a*exp(-j*omega))
    VALID CONDITION: |a| < 1, required for absolute summability and
    convergence.

(4) Complex Exponential (single frequency), periodic impulse train:
        exp(j*omega_0*n)   <--->   2*pi * Sum over k of
                                    delta(omega - omega_0 - 2*pi*k)

(5) Constant (DC) Sequence:
        1 (for all n)   <--->   2*pi * Sum over k of delta(omega - 2*pi*k)

(6) Cosine Sequence:
        cos(omega_0*n)  <--->  pi * Sum over k of
              [delta(omega - omega_0 - 2*pi*k) + delta(omega + omega_0 - 2*pi*k)]
